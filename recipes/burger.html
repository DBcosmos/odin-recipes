Burger

Burger Final Product

A burger is a sandwich consisting of a seasoned and cooked ground meat patty, typically beef, placed inside a sliced bun. It is commonly garnished with various condiments, vegetables, and cheese.

Ingredients

  • Ground meat
  • Burger buns
  • Condiments
  • Cheese
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare Meat:
    • Start by shaping the ground beef into patties. Season each patty with salt and pepper on both sides. You can make the patties according to your preferred size and thickness.
  2. Preheat the Grill:
    • Preheat your grill or a frying pan over medium-high heat. If using a grill, make sure it's clean and well-oiled to prevent sticking.
  3. Cook the patties:
    • Cook the burger patties on the preheated grill or pan. For medium-rare to medium burgers, cook for about 4-5 minutes per side. Adjust the cooking time based on your desired level of doneness.
  4. Add Cheese:
    • If you want cheese on your burgers, add a slice of cheese to each patty during the last minute of cooking. This allows the cheese to melt.
  5. Assemble Burger:
    • Once the patties are cooked to your liking, assemble the burgers. Place a cooked patty on the bottom half of a bun. Add condiments (ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise), cheese (if used), and vegetables. Top with the other half of the bun.
